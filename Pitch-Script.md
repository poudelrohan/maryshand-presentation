# Mary's Hand — 5-Minute Pitch Script

> **Read this word for word.** Practice it out loud a few times so it feels natural, but you can read directly from this during the pitch. Every line flows into the next.

---

## Slide 1 — Title

> "Hi, my name is Rohan from Bethune-Cookman University. Today I'm presenting Mary's Hand, a community app we built for Daytona Beach that connects people to food, events, and AI-powered meal planning."

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem

> "So food insecurity is a real issue in our area. And what that means is people not knowing where their next meal is coming from on a regular basis. Nearly 62,000 people in our county deal with that. There are neighborhoods right around our campus that are classified as food deserts, meaning there's no grocery store within a mile. And one in five children in our county don't have reliable access to food.
>
> Our campus sits right in the middle of all this. So we wanted to do something about it."

---

## Slide 3 — Research Evidence

> "We looked at six peer-reviewed studies and federal reports to understand the full picture. Here's what we found.
>
> Neighborhoods near our campus have incomes 48% below the state average. When families earn that little, they can't afford consistent meals, and that's why one in five children in our county are food insecure. A study across four HBCUs showed that nearly 73% of students experienced food insecurity too. And Johns Hopkins found that those students are 40% less likely to graduate. So this doesn't just affect health, it affects futures.
>
> Our university already responded by opening a food pantry called Mother Mary's Market with Second Harvest Food Bank. But there's still no app or tool that connects the broader community to surplus food or local volunteer events. That's the gap we saw."

---

## Slide 4 — Mary McLeod Bethune

> "And that brings me to why we named it Mary's Hand. For those who might not know, Dr. Mary McLeod Bethune founded our university back in 1904 with a dollar fifty and five students. She's one of the most influential civil rights leaders in American history and she dedicated her entire life to lifting her community through education and service.
>
> This app carries that mission forward. From her hand to yours."

---

## Slide 5 — CBL Framework

> "To build this, we followed Challenge-Based Learning. We started with a big idea which is community well-being. Not just for students, but for everyone around us. Our essential question was how can technology connect people to fight food insecurity together. And that challenge led us to build Mary's Hand."

---

## Slide 6 — What Mary's Hand Does

> "So what does the app actually do. There's a food sharing system where anyone in the community can post surplus food and anyone nearby can pick it up. There's a community events section that brings together volunteer opportunities from universities, nonprofits, and organizations around Daytona Beach. And there's Mary's Kitchen AI, which is an AI assistant that helps people plan meals and store food with what they already have.
>
> Now let me actually show you how it all works."

---

## Slide 7 — Live Demo

> **[Switch to Xcode Simulator]**
>
> "So I'm going to walk you through the actual app now."

**Home Page:**

> "This is the home page. You can see what's happening in the community at a glance. Recent food posts, upcoming events, and quick access to everything."

**Settings and Profile:**

> "Over here we have the profile and settings page where users can manage their account and their preferences."

**Experience and Gamification:**

> "We also built in a leveling system. The more you share food or volunteer at events, the more experience you earn. It keeps people engaged and encourages them to give back."

**Share Tab — Food:**

> "Now this is the share tab. Let me show how claiming food works first. You see something available, tap claim, and this confirmation bar fills up to prevent accidental taps. Once confirmed, you get directions to the pickup.
>
> Posting food is similar. You fill in what you have, add a photo, set a pickup location, and it goes live on the map."

**Events:**

> "Events work the same way. You can browse what's coming up, sign up for one, or post your own community event. I'll skim through this since the flow is similar to food."

**Map:**

> "Here's the community map. Let me zoom into my location. You can see food posts and events pinned around the area. This is useful because you can find what's closest to you and see what's happening in your neighborhood. You can also claim food and sign up for volunteering directly from here."

**Mary's Kitchen AI:**

> "And this is Mary's Kitchen AI. We used the OpenAI API for this. You can ask it anything about meal planning or food storage, and it gives you helpful answers. There are some prewritten questions here too. Let me pick one so you can see how it responds.
>
> And you'll notice this text-to-speech button right here, so the AI response can be read out loud for accessibility.
>
> Alright, let's go back to the slides."

---

## Slide 8 — Design and Tech

> "A quick look at the design and technology. We built everything in native SwiftUI with SwiftData, MapKit, and the OpenAI API. The color palette is BCU's maroon and gold, carried throughout the entire app.
>
> The design uses glass morphism with an aurora background. It's actually the same effect running behind these slides right now. We followed Apple's Human Interface Guidelines to keep everything feeling like a real iOS app."

---

## Slide 9 — Accessibility

> "Since this app is meant for the whole community, accessibility was something we took seriously. We have full VoiceOver support on every screen. There's a dyslexic font option. Text scales up with Dynamic Type for anyone who needs larger text. We have dark mode, haptic feedback, and the AI reads responses out loud.
>
> These are just some of the 18 plus features we included. If this app is supposed to serve the whole community, it needs to actually work for the whole community."

---

## Slide 10 — Impact

> "We're starting right here in Daytona Beach, serving the neighborhoods around us. From there, the goal is to expand across Volusia County and eventually bring this to other HBCU communities facing the same challenges."

---

## Slide 11 — Close

> "Mary's Hand. Your community, always within reach. Thank you, I'd love to take any questions."
