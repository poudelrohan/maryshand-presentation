## HBCU Food Insecurity (Strongest Sources)

### 1. GAO Federal Report (2024)
- **Title:** Estimated Eligibility and Receipt among Food Insecure College Students
- **Org:** U.S. Government Accountability Office
- **Key Stat:** **38% of HBCU students** are food insecure, vs 20% at non-minority-serving institutions
- **URL:** https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-24-107074

### 2. Hope Center #RealCollege HBCU Report (2021)
- **Title:** Basic Needs Insecurity at HBCUs
- **Org:** Temple University Hope Center
- **Key Stat:** **46% of HBCU students** reported food insecurity in the prior month (survey of ~5,000 students across 14 HBCUs)
- **URL:** https://hope.temple.edu/sites/hope/files/media/document/HBCU_FINAL.pdf

### 3. Duke et al. — 4 HBCUs Study (2021)
- **Title:** Prevalence of Food Insecurity Among Students Attending Four HBCUs
- **Journal:** Journal of American College Health (PMC)
- **Key Stat:** **72.9% of students** at four southeastern HBCUs reported some level of food insecurity
- **DOI:** 10.1080/07448481.2021.1877144
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8521625/

### 4. HBCUs in Food Deserts (2022)
- **Title:** Examining Disparities in Food Access Between HBCUs and Non-HBCUs
- **Journal:** American Journal of Health Promotion
- **Key Stat:** HBCUs have **104% greater odds** of being located in low food access areas (OR=2.04)
- **DOI:** 10.1177/08901171211024412
- **URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/08901171211024412

### 5. BMC Public Health — HBCU Barriers (2024)
- **Title:** Barriers to Use of Food Security Initiatives in an HBCU
- **Key Stat:** Food pantry awareness was only **27.9%** and utilization just **8.1%** despite 63.5% food insecurity
- **URL:** https://bmcpublichealth.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1186/s12889-024-20627-1

## Volusia County / Daytona Beach (Local Data)

### 6. UF/IFAS Extension — Volusia Food Deserts (2020)
- **Title:** Increasing Accessibility to Fresh Produce in Urban Food Deserts in Volusia County
- **Key Stat:** Derbyshire community (Daytona Beach 32117) has median income **48% below Florida's median** with no grocery stores within 1 mile
- **URL:** https://blogs.ifas.ufl.edu/extension/2020/04/11/increasing-accessibility-to-fresh-produce-in-urban-food-deserts-in-volusia-county-florida/

### 7. USDA Food Access Research Atlas
- **Key Stat:** Multiple census tracts in Volusia County classified as **low-income, low-access** (food deserts), including neighborhoods near BCU
- **URL:** https://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/food-access-research-atlas/go-to-the-atlas

### 8. Feeding America — Volusia County
- **Key Stat:** **61,990 residents** face food insecurity in Volusia County; 1 in 5 children are food insecure
- **URL:** https://map.feedingamerica.org/county/2017/overall/florida/county/volusia

## Bethune-Cookman University (Direct)

### 9. BCU Opens Food Pantry (2025)
- **Title:** Bethune-Cookman University Latest Florida College to Add Food Pantry
- **Org:** Central Florida Public Media
- **Key Stat:** BCU opened "Mother Mary's Market" food pantry in partnership with Second Harvest Food Bank, acknowledging that ~38% of HBCU students are food insecure

## Academic Performance Impact

### 10. Johns Hopkins — Graduation Rates (2021)
- **Key Stat:** Food-insecure students are **40% less likely to graduate** and **60% less likely** to earn graduate degrees
- **URL:** https://publichealth.jhu.edu/2021/food-insecurity-during-college-years-linked-to-lower-graduation-rate

### 11. Systematic Review — GPA Impact (2026)
- **Journal:** Current Nutrition Reports (Springer)
- **Key Stat:** **92% of 24 studies** found food insecurity associated with lower GPA
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s13668-026-00744-6

## Food Waste at Universities

### 12. PMC — University Food Waste (2021)
- **Key Stat:** Students waste an estimated **112 lbs of food per student per year**; campuses account for ~12% of US food waste
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8000212/

## Technology Solutions (Precedent)

### 13. LoboEats App — UNM (2023)
- **Key Finding:** Peer-to-peer food sharing app at University of New Mexico posts surplus food with real-time notifications, destigmatizing access
- **URL:** https://news.unm.edu/news/introducing-the-loboeats-app-addressing-food-waste-and-insecurity-on-central-campus
